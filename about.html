ABOUT

INDIGENOUS is not just a fashion magazine. It is a reflection of identity, a documentation of culture, and a voice for expressions that often remain unseen. It exists to bridge the gap between heritage and contemporary fashion, where stories are not only worn but lived.

Founded by Bharath Kumar from Rayalaseema University, INDIGENOUS was born from a deep curiosity and passion for fashion. Not driven by trends, but by observation, Bharath sees fashion as a language — one that speaks through fabric, texture, posture, and presence.

As a fashion enthusiast, his vision is not to replicate global aesthetics, but to bring forward the authenticity of Indian fashion in its rawest and most original form. From street styles to regional influences, from modern silhouettes to traditional roots — every layer tells a story of where we come from and where we are heading.

INDIGENOUS stands at the intersection of Western influence and Indian culture, not as a blend, but as a dialogue. A space where both worlds coexist, challenge each other, and create something entirely new.

This platform is built to showcase real perspectives — not perfection, but authenticity. Not imitation, but identity. Every frame, every edition, and every feature is an attempt to redefine how Indian fashion is seen, understood, and experienced.

This is more than fashion. This is a narrative of origin, a representation of individuality, and a movement towards owning our cultural voice.

This is INDIGENOUS.
